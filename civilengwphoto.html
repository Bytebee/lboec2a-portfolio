What is a Civil Engineer?

Civil engineering is a professional engineering discipline that deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, including public works such as roads, bridges, canals, dams, airports, sewage systems, pipelines, structural components of buildings, and railways.They are responsible for ensuring that public structures are safe to use, tough, and made to last. While Architects draw up ideas for buildings, Civil Engineers work on execution, planning and ensuring that the structure is safe to use for the public.

How to be a Civil Engineer? What are the steps?

  • Complete 4-5 years of Undergraduate Education
  • Pass all Subjects to progress through the years.
  • Take organizations if needed, to help in your resume.
  • Apply for internships in your Graduating years.
  • Make sure that you have a good research and thesis background (good research papers, thesis, etc.).
  • Start planning what to do after graduation to ensure great chances of landing a good job.
  • Once you pass all subjects throughout 4-5 years, you can obtain your degree to be a Civil Engineer.
  • Study for Board Exam to earn license. Your license helps you ensure that you get into a good job and you can work for big companies.
  • If you pass the board exam, you are qualified to be a licensed Civil Engineer.
  • Prepare to apply for jobs and start practicing for interviews.
  • Prepare needed documents for job applications, if needed. Here are some examples:
    • Personal info (Birth Certificate, Driver's License, Engineer's license, etc.)
    • Resume, make sure that it is concise and straight to the point.
  • Repeat application process for each job until you get accepted.
  • Congratulations! You are now a licensed Civil Engineer who landed a great job.
